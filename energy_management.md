# Energy Management
### 1: Activities for the Calm Quadrant
* Engaging in hobbies without any agenda to perform or improve.
* Having lighthearted conversations with loved ones.
* Practicing meditation, doing nothing, or taking physical rest.
* Doing light leisure activities like walking in nature or reading.
* Grounding activities like spending time in the sunlight or walking barefoot.

### 2: Triggers for the Stress Quadrant
* Tackling tight deadlines or high-pressure situations at work.
* Learning a completely new, difficult skill or subject.
* Engaging in high-intensity physical workouts or sports.
* Feeling overwhelmed by a lack of structure or daily routine.

### 3: Identifying the Excitement Quadrant
* Experiencing a strong sense of joy, and physical energy.
* Entering a state where we completely lose track of time.
* Feeling highly motivated, focused, and eager to take on a challenge.
* Engaging deeply in a passion project without feeling drained.

### 4: Sleep is Your Superpower
* Sleep deprivation prevents the brain from absorbing and making new memories.
* Disruption of deep sleep is directly linked to aging, cognitive decline, and Alzheimer's disease.
* Losing just one hour of sleep significantly increases the risk of heart attacks and cardiovascular issues.
* Lack of sleep drastically drops immune cell activity, increasing vulnerability to illness and various cancers.

### 5: Ideas to Implement for Better Sleep
* **Regularity:** Go to bed and wake up at the exact same time every day, even on weekends.
* **Temperature:** Keep your bedroom cool.
* **Environment:** Dim the lights of room.
* **Substances:** Avoid alcohol and caffeine, as they heavily disrupt sleep quality.
* **Napping:** Avoid daytime naps.

### 6: Brain-Changing Benefits of Exercise
* A single workout instantly boosts mood by elevating dopamine, serotonin, and adrenaline levels.
* Immediate exercise improves focus, attention, and reaction times for up to two hours.

* Regular physical activity permanently strengthens and enhances long-term attention and focus.
* Exercise acts as a protective shield for the brain.

### 7: Steps to Exercise More
* Aim for  3 to 4 times a week for at least 20 minutes per session.
* Integrate movement into daily life, such as taking the stairs, power-walking.
* Experiment with different types of classes (yoga, swimming, dance) to find what keeps us motivated.
* Treat exercise as a non-negotiable part of our routine.
