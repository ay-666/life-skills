# Good Practices for Software Development

### Question 1: Which points were new to you?
* **Sandbox Setup Sharing:** Recreating complex bugs in sandbox environments (like CodePen) to share the entire setup, rather than relying  on error logs or isolated snippets. 
* **Proactive Alignment:** Manually documenting and circulating meeting requirements for immediate feedback when formal tracking tools aren't being utilized by the team.

### Question 2: Which area do you think you need to improve on? What are your ideas to make progress in that area?
**Area for Improvement:** * "Doing things with 100% involvement". Managing context switching is a challenge, especially when balancing core development work with managing community forums or video content creation.

**Ideas for Progress:**
* **Strict Time-Blocking:** Dedicate specific, uninterrupted blocks of time solely for complex architectural tasks, such as configuring backend environments or processing large datasets.
* **Digital Hygiene:** Actively utilize app-blocking tools during core hours to restrict access to analytics dashboards, social media, and community management platforms.
* **Batched Communication:** When stuck on code, consolidate all troubleshooting steps, GitHub Gists, and system logs into one comprehensive message for the team, rather than sending multiple scattered updates.
